Steak with Red Wine Sauce

Raiting:

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins

Nutrition

Calories 535 kcal
Fat 34g
Fibre 3g
Protein 30g

Ingredients

  • 2 steaks, 1 to 2 inches thick
  • Kosher salt
  • Extra virgin olive oil or canola oil
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 2 cups full bodied, dry red wine
  • Black pepper, to taste

Methods

  1. Remove Tough Parts: Trim away any tough or gristly sections of the steaks. If there's a chain attached that you prefer not to serve, remove it and reserve it for another dish.
  2. Bring the Steaks to Room Temperature: Rest Before Cooking: Allow the steaks to sit at room temperature for 30 to 90 minutes, depending on their thickness. This ensures even cooking. Before cooking, pat the steaks dry with paper towels, brush with oil, and sprinkle with salt.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to a cast iron pan and heat it on high for about 1 minute. Swirl the oil around to evenly coat the bottom of the pan. Put the steaks into the hot pan, then lower the heat to medium or medium-high, depending on your stove. Let the steaks sear without moving them for 3 to 6 minutes, depending on thickness.
  4. Turn the steaks and cook for 2 to 5 more minutes. Check doneness with a meat thermometer (120°F for rare, 130°F for medium rare, 140°F for medium) or use the finger test.
  5. Remove the steaks, cover with foil, and let them rest while you make the sauce.
  6. Make the Red Wine Sauce: Pour wine into the pan and boil over high heat until reduced by three-quarters (about 10 minutes). Add the resting steak juices. Turn off the heat once the wine stops bubbling. Add butter gradually, one tablespoon at a time, and swirl until melted.Season with salt. Serve the sauce with the steak, topping with freshly ground black pepper
