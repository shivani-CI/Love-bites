    Chocolate Fondant

    Raiting:

    Prep Time: 25 mins
    Cook Time: 25 mins

    Nutrition

    Calories 581 kcal
    Fat 9g
    Fibre 0g
    Protein 9g

    Ingredients

    • 50g melted butter, for brushing
    • Cocoa powder, for dusting
    • 200g good-quality dark chocolate, chopped into small pieces
    • 200g butter, cut into small pieces
    • 200g golden caster sugar
    • 4 eggs and 4 egg yolks
    • 200g plain flour
    • Caramel sauce and vanilla ice cream or orange sorbet, for serving

    Methods

    1. Prepare your moulds by brushing the insides with melted butter (50g total) using upward strokes. Chill the moulds in the fridge or freezer. Once chilled, brush with more melted butter, then coat with cocoa powder, ensuring even coverage. Tap out excess cocoa and repeat with each mould.
    2. Melt 200g chopped dark chocolate and 200g chopped butter together in a bowl set over a pan of simmering water. Stir until smooth, then remove from heat and let cool for about 10 minutes.
    3. In a separate bowl, whisk 4 eggs and 4 egg yolks with 200g golden caster sugar until thick and pale, and the whisk leaves a trail. Sift 200g plain flour into the mixture and beat until combined.
    4. Gradually add the melted chocolate to the egg mixture in thirds, beating well between each addition, until fully combined into a loose cake batter.
    5. Pour the batter into a jug and evenly divide it between the prepared moulds. The fondants can be frozen for up to a month. Chill for at least 20 minutes or overnight. If baking from frozen, add 5 extra minutes to the cooking time.
    6. Preheat the oven to 200°C/180°C fan/gas 6. Place the moulds on a baking tray and bake for 10-12 minutes, until the tops form a crust and they start to pull away from the sides. Remove from the oven and let sit for 1 minute before turning out.
    7. Gently loosen the fondants by moving the tops so they separate from the sides. Carefully tip each fondant onto your hand to check it's loosened, then return them to the moulds until ready to serve.
    8. On each plate, create a spiral of caramel sauce starting from the center. Prepare all plates before proceeding.
    9. Place a fondant in the center of each plate. Using a large spoon dipped in hot water, scoop a quenelle of ice cream and place it on top of the fondant.
    10. Serve immediately. Repeat with the remaining fondants.
